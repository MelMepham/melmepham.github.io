Reflections

Reflections into the journey of EDA through the musings of Sherref

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

I think education takes a whole lot of different forms, and I love how DBC and EDA are taking a fresh and interesting approach to teaching really relivant skills in an interesting and creative way.

What are your impressions?

impression

1. a strong effect produced on the intellect, feelings, conscience, etc.

2. the first and immediate effect of an experience or perception upon the mind; sensation.

My impression is that this experience as a whole has the ability to be really transformative, providing I give it the time and attention it needs to be such an experience.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I am all for vunerability, and being realy open and honest about my short falls and sucesses so I think the learning environment will be one that I will thrive in. I will probably struggle to begin with forming connections with other students, I have had some pretty traumatic learning environments in the past and it has lead to a response of isolation. I am really excited about different approaches to learning and working industries and under normal circumstances I wouldn't go to an education provider to learn code.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

No. My expectations of EDA have not changed after watching that video, maybe the realisation of the amount of group work that will be involved, but it is a hurdle I am willing to tackle!

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I think if I wasn't feeling nervous right now I would be too confident... I think this is a silly question I don't know why you would enrol to learn a new set of skills if it didn't excite you.
