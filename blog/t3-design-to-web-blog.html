DESIGN TO WEB

What are some easy ways to move yout designs from ideas to actual webpages?

Tuesday the 14th of November

As we talked about in the last post, responsive design is really important in todays day and age because of the multitude of different devices people look at content with. One really easy way to make sure your content looks the best on the smallest screen size is to use a technique called “mobile first design”. It is pretty self-explanatory. Basically make sure you design looks good on the smallest screen size and then work up from there! that means your info will be more accessible to a wider audience and you will keep the design integrity!

There are so many people now working in the tech industry, people are bound to run in and have to solve the same types of problems, there is two ways to overcome this. Either you write your own code, or you look at what other people have already written and use what we call ‘Frameworks”. These are basically libraries of code people have written to deal with similar issues. check out this website that I have used, I have used a framework called Skeleton to create a nice responsive grid system without having to write a bunch of code myself! Check it out, resize my page and see what you think.

Before I started designing my website in my text editor, I sketched what is commonly called a ‘wireframe’ in the industry. Basically its a sketch to show what the skeleton or basic grid of my page will look like this helped me with the initial stages of having to figure out what things needed to be grouped and to make sure my design would look good on both mobile and desktop.

My wireframe was the first sketch I did, which was bad design on my part but I really just wanted to sink my teeth into playing with what I could do! Check out my initial sketches.
